This skill provides 106 cognitive biases and UX psychology principles from Growth.design, organized into 4 categories based on how the brain processes information. Use it to make design decisions backed by behavioral science rather than gut feeling.

## When this activates

This skill complements `frontend-design` (which handles aesthetics). Use it when:
- Designing **prototypes, wireframes, or user flows**
- Working on **onboarding, pricing pages, checkout flows**
- Building **forms, dashboards, navigation, or notification systems**
- Reviewing designs for **conversion, engagement, or retention**
- Any UX decision where **user behavior** matters

## How to use the references

1. **Start with `scenario-index.md`** — find your scenario (onboarding, pricing, forms, etc.) and get 3-6 recommended principles
2. **Open the relevant category file** — read only the specific principles you need
3. **Apply selectively** — pick 2-4 principles max per design decision, don't overload

Never apply all principles at once. The goal is precision, not quantity.
