  19. WORLD BUILDING (PART V): MICRO-DOSING YOUR AUDIENCE

What's the least stressful way of using social media for your art?

How do you​ keep your audience entertained without getting burnt out yourself?

You use a concept as old as the streets:

Micro-dosing.

First, you have to figure out two things:


1. What is the art "experience" you're selling?

You answered that in WORLD BUILDING (PART II): HOW TO CREATE YOUR POWERFUL ONLINE EXPERIENCE


2. Where will people go to get that whole experience?

You answered that in WORLD BUILDING (PART III): YOUR WORLD VS. YOUR TOOLS.


With these answers, everything else can fall into place. 

Every platform that is NOT YOUR WORLD is now a potential TOOL to bring people into your experience.

There are no rules.

One woman's tools can be another woman's whole experience:

Examples?

Lululemon is using Tiktok as a tool. But Charli D'Amelio is using Tiktok as her entire experience. 

Halsey is using YouTube as a tool. In contrast, Emma Chamberlain uses YouTube as her full experience.

Podcasts can be a tool, or they can be the whole experience. 

Onlyfans can be a tool or the whole experience.

So, once again... Figure out:

1. What is your "experience"?

2. Where do people go to consume it?

Whatever you come up with is what we call your WORLD.

Every other platform that isn't your world = ATTRACTION TOOLS.

But the question that haunts so many creatives is...

HOW do you use these TOOLS to get people into your WORLD?

You do what the best drug dealers in the world do:

You micro-dose them.


MICRO-DOSE = A FREE SMALL TASTE THAT CONTAINS THE WHOLE EXPERIENCE.

I mean... Isn't art a drug? Doesn't the best poetry, music, painting, writing, and dance... INTOXICATE us?

So wouldn't that kind of make you the "pusher"?

I'm kidding. But I'm also not.

When David's Tea or innovative Whole Foods brands want to get people addicted to their product, they give you a SAMPLE.

EVERY MICRO-DOSE SHOULD CONTAIN THE ESSENCE OF YOUR ART.

"But, Father Bronques, what if my experience can't be 'bite-sized'?" (It always can. Be creative. But, still I'll humor you here.)

Give them an emotional microdose. Show other people experiencing your world. Give them that "I'll have what she's having" feeling by using the power of video. "Demonstration" is a powerful way of making people feel like they're ALMOST there. It's more potent than... giving them a coupon or a flyer.

But remember... Whatever you share to draw them in needs to be satisfying all on its own.

That's good business.


THE BIG MISTAKE EVERYONE MAKES?

If you want to feel frustrated or waste energy... Give people enticing but unrelated micro-doses. Yes, it will attract them to your experience. But they won't stick around. Or they'll only stick around for the micro-doses.

If you were an Italian restaurant, would you give people a discount at the apple store? Or would you reel them in for life by giving them a bite of the main dish? 

If you're a singer? Would you give them a thirst trap? Or would you give them a taste of your music? 

It's easy to "trick" people into giving you attention-- But how satisfying is that if those people aren't committed to your WORLD?

And yes, sometimes it's expensive to give your customers some of your "stash" for free. But if you look at it in terms of the "lifetime value" of your die-hard fans...

It's more than worth it.

Why do you think Ben and Jerry's and Starbucks have days where they give their main thing away for free?

"Lifetime value" of their die-hard fans.

THE "TASTE" SHOULD CONTAIN THE WHOLE EXPERIENCE.


HOW TO DISH IT OUT - MAKE THE MICRO-DOSE FIT THE PLATFORM

1. Figure out where your potential fans are hiding (platform).

2. Figure out why your audience uses that platform.

3. Create a "taste of your 'experience'" that fits with the reason why they're on this platform. Create "platform worthy micro-doses" that would be like catnip to a kitty.

I know, I know... You always want examples:

People go to Instagram and Tiktok for entertainment. So entertain them. Do they want awesome videos? Give them awesome videos. Do they want a heartfelt connection? Give them genuine connections. Give them a taste of your art in the platform's format that contains your whole experience within it.

But don't limit yourself to only social media. There are millions of attraction tools available to you.

Even art forms can be attractions tools.

Take music, for example. 

Movie producers use music as a tool to get people to see their movies all the time. They use music in movie soundtracks and trailers to attract fans into their movie world.

Music is a TOOL even for musicians!

MUSIC is the ultimate micro-dose.

A music career today is not about selling songs. It's about getting people HOOKED to songs, so they want the whole experience of the artist. That's why music artists who haven't built a world outside of their released songs have... nothing.

Give them that "music micro-dose" (hit song), and a year later, they've spent $80 on a concert ticket and $150 on merch, and they watch all your pay-per-views on VEEPS.

That's how you create a career with your art.

Know what business you're in.

Know what "your world" is.

(Your world = your experience + where people go to consume it).

Treat everything else as an attraction tool to get them there. 

Attraction Tools like SOCIAL MEDIA: Instagram, Tiktok, Twitch, Twitter, Snapchat, and Facebook.

Or attraction tools like TRADITIONAL MEDIA: Podcasts, Onlyfans, Music, TV, radio, magazines, and blogs.

Treat every appearance you make in traditional media as a micro-dose of your experience. You do that by staying on message in interviews and reinforcing the themes that drew in your biggest fans. 


THE TIERS OF WORLD-BUILDING (A RECAP FOR Part I to V)

$ Free

Microdose of the experience.

Music: streaming

Painter: sketches, showing yourself painting on youtube

Dancer: American Ballet performing in the park


$$ People pay for your brain / intellectual property

For a musician, this might be vinyl, merch etc...

For a painter, this might be buying the actual painting.

For a dancer, this could be dance classes, dancing in someone's music video.


$$$ People pay for your physical presence / pay more because it doesn't scale

For a musician, this is a live show. Or a private performance for a prince in Dubai.

For a painter, this is a commissioned piece (Warhol made millions doing this)

For a dancer, this would be creating choreography for someone.


Ready to build your "world"?
