Looking at Don Norman’s seven fundamental design principles

_Quick writer’s note: I’ve edited the article for clarity as of May 23rd, 2025. Enjoy!_

We designers love principles. They guide us in creating valuable products and shaping meaningful experiences. Of all the books and digital spaces filled with design wisdom, I always reflect on _Don Norman’s_ timeless **seven fundamental design principles** from his best-selling book [_The Design of Everyday Things_](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/840.The_Design_of_Everyday_Things) _._

Norman often frames his design principles as questions to help us evaluate usability from a user’s perspective — and by answering each one, we uncover design strategies that make our products, services, and systems efficient, effective, and delightful.

To help you explore these principles in action, I’ve paired each one with examples from both classic and modern products. Let’s begin with the first: discoverability.

## 1\. Discoverability

_How can we make the possibilities easy for users to discover?_
